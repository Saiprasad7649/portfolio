Excel Dashboard

Hospital Analytics & Performance Optimization

An end-to-end Excel dashboard built on 500 real patient records spanning 12 months, designed to give hospital administrators a single view of operational efficiency, financial performance, and resource utilization.

Problem Identified
Operational Blind Spot

Hospital administrators lacked a consolidated view connecting patient volume, bed utilization, case complexity, and revenue — making it impossible to pinpoint where inefficiencies were costing the most or which specialties drove the highest financial return.

Key Insights Uncovered
Revenue is specialty-driven, not volume-driven. General Surgery and Orthopaedics contributed ~31% of total revenue despite not being the highest-volume departments — signaling where case complexity pays off.
Payer mix is dangerously concentrated. 85.6% insurance-funded vs. 14.4% corporate — a single-payer reliance that creates financial risk if contract terms shift.
Bed occupancy (28.2%) is well below optimal thresholds (75–85%), indicating significant capacity underutilization and a missed revenue opportunity.
CMI rises linearly with severity (1.006 → 1.076 across levels 1–3), confirming coding alignment — but showing headroom to improve CMI capture for complex cases.
Revenue peaked in February ($1.29M) and collapsed in December ($242K) — a 79% drop signaling seasonal demand variation that staffing and budgets should anticipate.
Business Impact

Provided hospital leadership with a single, always-current operational view — replacing ad-hoc reporting with a structured dashboard that surfaces the link between clinical complexity, resource use, and financial performance. Findings directly inform specialty investment priorities, payer contract strategy, and seasonal workforce planning.
